## How it was decided

A 10-agent workflow: 5 visual analysts (one per image batch) + 1 current-site token
reader → 1 synthesis → 3 adversarial verifiers (WCAG contrast math on 13 pairs — all
pass AA; a fidelity skeptic re-viewing the source images; a web-craft skeptic).
Both skeptics returned "needs-adjustment" with fixes, all adopted:

- Keep `--brick` **oxblood** (her boards ground the orange in wine tones); bright
  poppy became its own `--poppy` token instead of overwriting it.
- Add `--blush` (a co-lead accent across the boards that synthesis missed).
- Merge the proposed two cobalts (1.16:1 apart — imperceptible) into one.
- Blue stays **graphic edge, not field**: her boards have no large solid blue; cap
  one cobalt band per page (the natural state of every current page) + footer.
- Photo overlays derive from desaturated navy-indigo `#202A55`, not raw cobalt —
  full chroma casts purple on the coastal photos. Verified by screenshot.
- Hero names stay cream over photos (poppy script at 3:1 is illegible); bright
  poppy type is allowed only at display sizes on cream.
- Links scoped per surface: burnt poppy on cream, cream-underlined on cobalt
  (burnt poppy on cobalt is 1.9:1 — never).
- Plate-rim button needs three inset shadow stops (fill-colored gap), not two.
- No hover-only state signals: the nav's active dot is `aria-current`-driven;
  hover glows are decorative desktop garnish.

## Color-flow fix (2026-07-16) — "quiet chrome, wine bookends" — LIVE ON PROD

Patrick's verdict on the sunset nav: too jarring, no flow, "the main top bar fade is
not great." A 5-designer / 3-judge workflow converged on **Quiet Chrome: Wine
Bookends** (won client-fit + craft lenses; the cream-nav alternatives lost on the
light-bar-over-dark-hero value slam). The rules now in force:

- **No gradients anywhere.** Nav = solid grape fizz `#3F0013` (same wine as the
  footer, so every page is bookended in one pigment) with a faint cream inset
  hairline; the marigold glint is gone. Photo scrims are single flat tints.
- **Temperature rule:** WARM photos (the tent) get a flat grape-fizz scrim
  `rgba(48,0,15,.46)` (`.section--scene-warm` = `.55` where text sits on it);
  WATER photos keep the flat bluebell dusk `rgba(58,63,82,.68)` — the palette's
  only blue, and it may only ever border cream, never the wine chrome. The same
  photo must never appear in two temperatures (registry header follows the hero).
- **Sweet Cream surfaces:** `.card`/`.method` sit on `--cream-2` with an
  oxblood-tinted hairline border; links inside them are oxblood `--brick` (6.6:1 —
  terracotta fails AA on that tint at 4.2:1; the rule must stay BELOW the
  `.section--cream` link rule in source order, and must exclude `.card--olive`,
  whose links stay cream — brick on olive-deep is ~1.5:1).
- **Signature extensions:** scallop seam added where cream meets a photo band
  (`.section--cream + .section--scene::after` — `::before` carries the overlay);
  the nav RSVP pill wears a miniature plate-rim double ring.
- `--overlay-deep-rgb` retired (unused once scrims went flat).
- Back pocket (from the judges): scallop-seam.svg as a CSS `mask` filled with the
  lower band's color, to seam dark-on-dark joints (dusk header → olive band on
  schedule/travel); marigold baseline as the escalation if the wine bar reads dead.
